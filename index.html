China is about finding common ground to maintain control over the expression of opinions, but without letting them act on these opinions.

In the book "censored distraction and diversion inside china's great firewall" 3 methods are described on how the government controls the internet

Fear and threats

Effective immediately but there's a reaction of people trying to seek out information if they feel the government is sensitive about it being seen. Only used for protestors and journalists

Flooding

Drowns up critical thoughts in a sea of pro gov messages using internet trolls and a load of opposing article

Friction

  1. Increase the cost of finding information
  2. Make parts of applications slower
  3. Ban website with the knowledge of existing VPNs
  4. This strategy is most popular and relies on our lack of patience
  5. The average citizen will just use the service that "just works" and mostly it is government controlled
